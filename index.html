Why Are Subheadings Important?

Good subheadings help your reader:
  • Find information quickly ‚Äì readers can scan your page
  • Understand your research ‚Äì each section has a clear focus
  • Stay interested ‚Äì visual variety keeps attention
  • See your thinking ‚Äì shows how you organised your ideas
Important: Your subheadings should be specific. Instead of "Research", write what the research is about. For example: "Traditional Japanese Textile Patterns" or "Sustainable Fabric Sources".

Key Terms You Will Use

Generative Fill

An AI tool in Photoshop. You write a description (prompt), and it creates an image for you.

Example prompt: "Create a masking tape texture"

Prompt

The words you type to tell the AI what to create. Better prompts give better results.

Tip: Be specific! Include colours, textures, and styles.

Contextual Taskbar

A toolbar that appears when you select something in Photoshop. It shows the Generative Fill button.

Content-Aware Fill

A Photoshop tool that fills areas by looking at the surrounding image. Good for backgrounds.

Step-by-Step Process In-Class

Setting Up Your Document

1
Create a New A3 Document

Open Photoshop and create a new document:

  • Go to File ‚Üí New
  • Set size to A3
  • Orientation: Landscape
  • Resolution: 300 dpi
  • Colour Mode: CMYK
Insert screenshot: New Document settings in Photoshop
2
Create a Main Rectangle

Draw a rectangle for your page background:

  • Select the Rectangle Tool (U)
  • Draw a large rectangle on your canvas
  • This will be your main content area
Insert screenshot: Rectangle Tool selected and rectangle drawn
3
Paste Into A3 Document
  • Copy your rectangle (Ctrl/Cmd + C)
  • Paste it into your A3 document (Ctrl/Cmd + V)
  • Position it where you want your main content area
4
Load Selection

Select the rectangle shape:

  • Hold Ctrl/Cmd and click on the rectangle layer thumbnail
  • Or go to Select ‚Üí Load Selection
  • You will see marching ants around your rectangle
Insert screenshot: Load Selection with marching ants visible
5
Inverse Selection

Select the area outside your rectangle:

  • Go to Select ‚Üí Inverse
  • Now the outside area is selected
  • This is where your background texture will go
6
Apply Content-Aware Fill

Create a textured sketchbook-style background:

  • Go to Edit ‚Üí Content-Aware Fill
  • Adjust settings if needed
  • Click OK to apply

Result: Your page now has a natural, textured background.

TASK 2: Create Your Design Page

In this task, you will use your Generative Fill skills to create a complete research design page.

What Makes a Good Research Page?

Clear Subheadings
  • Tell the reader what each section is about
  • Be specific, not general
  • Use visual backgrounds to make them stand out
Research Content
  • Include factual information
  • Show you have investigated your topic
  • Use your own words (not copy-paste)
Visual Balance
  • Combine images, backgrounds, and text
  • Leave some white space
  • Make it easy to read
Professional Look
  • Consistent style throughout
  • Aligned elements
  • Good colour choices

Subheading Guidelines

How to Write Good Subheadings:
  • Be specific: Instead of "Research", write "Traditional Kimono Construction Methods"
  • Highlight your focus: Each subheading shows what you are investigating
  • Keep it short: 3-6 words is usually enough
  • Use your subheadings to tell a story: They should guide the reader through your research

Example: Good vs. Weak Subheadings

‚ùå Weak Subheadings

  • "Research"
  • "Images"
  • "Information"
  • "Artist"

These are too general. They don't tell the reader anything specific.

‚úì Good Subheadings

  • "Shibori Dyeing Techniques"
  • "Natural Dye Colour Palette"
  • "Sustainable Fabric Sources"
  • "Issey Miyake's Pleating Process"

These are specific. The reader knows exactly what each section covers.

Building Your Design Page

Page Structure

1
Plan Your Layout

Before you start in Photoshop:

  • Decide how many sections you need
  • Write your subheading titles
  • Plan where images and text will go
  • Sketch a rough layout on paper
2
Create Your Background
  • Use the Content-Aware Fill technique from Task 1
  • Choose a style that fits your research topic
  • Keep it subtle ‚Äì it should not distract from content
3
Add Your Subheadings
  • Create rectangles for each subheading
  • Apply Generative Fill with appropriate prompts
  • Choose textures that work together
  • Add your text on top
4
Add Research Content
  • Place your images
  • Write short paragraphs of factual information
  • Make sure text is readable (good contrast)
  • Include source information where needed
5
Review and Refine
  • Check alignment of all elements
  • Make sure the page is balanced
  • Read through all text for errors
  • Ask: Can a reader understand my research easily?
